Understanding Pips

A pip is the smallest price move that a currency pair can make. For most pairs, this is 0.0001. For JPY pairs, it's 0.01.

Example: If EUR/USD moves from 1.0850 to 1.0851, that's a 1 pip movement.

Calculation: Pip Value = (0.0001 / Exchange Rate) × Trade Size

Spread & Commission

The spread is the difference between the bid and ask price. This is how most brokers make money.

Current EUR/USD Spread: 0.6 pips

Why it matters: Lower spreads mean less cost to enter a trade. Our recommended brokers offer spreads as low as 0.0 pips.

Risk Management

Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade. This ensures you can survive losing streaks.

Example: With a $5,000 account, don't risk more than $50-$100 per trade.

Position Size Formula: (Account Balance × Risk %) ÷ (Stop Loss in Pips × Pip Value)

Risk/Reward Ratio

A good risk/reward ratio ensures that your winning trades outweigh your losses.

Example: With a 1:3 risk/reward ratio, you only need to be right 25% of the time to break even.

Calculation: (Take Profit - Entry) ÷ (Entry - Stop Loss)
