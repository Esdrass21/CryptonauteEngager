Understanding Pips

A pip is the smallest price move that a currency pair can make. For most pairs, this is 0.0001. For JPY pairs, it's 0.01.

Example: If EUR/USD moves from 1.0850 to 1.0851, that's a 1 pip movement.

Spread & Commission

The spread is the difference between the bid and ask price. This is how most brokers make money.

Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade. This ensures you can survive losing streaks.

Example: With a $5,000 account, don't risk more than $50-$100 per trade.
